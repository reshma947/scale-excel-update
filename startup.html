Startup India Registration

The Startup India Registration initiative, launched in 2016 by the Government of India, aims to transform India into an innovation hub. Recognized by the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT), startups enjoy benefits like tax exemptions, funding, and simplified compliance, fostering innovation, job creation, and economic growth.

Eligible startups (under 10 years old with turnover below ₹100 crore) gain cost reductions on IP filings, fast-tracked patent processing, and tender access without prior experience. Financial support from schemes like the Fund of Funds and Seed Fund Scheme, along with mentorship and networking opportunities, boosts growth. Vakilsearch simplifies the registration process, enabling startups to scale efficiently.

What is Startup India Scheme ?

The Startup India Scheme is a flagship initiative launched on January 16, 2016, by the Government of India to transform the country into a hub for innovation and entrepreneurship. The program's mission is to shift India from a nation of job seekers to one of job creators. Managed by the Startup India Team under the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT), it offers startups financial support, mentorship, and favourable regulations to eliminate barriers and accelerate growth.

Objectives of the Startup India Scheme

The primary goal of Startup India is to enhance product development, innovation, and job opportunities in India.The three main pillars of Startup India include:

1. Funding Support and Incentives:Providing financial assistance and incentives to startups across the country.

2. Industry-Academia Partnership and Incubation: Fostering collaboration between industry and academic institutions, and supporting incubation programs.

3. Simplification and Handholding: Streamlining processes and offering guidance to help startups navigate challenges and scale effectively.

Startup India Registration Eligibility

The Startup India eligibility criteria cover three broad areas: turnover, startup age, innovation requirements, scalability potential, and sector-specific conditions. Here is a detailed outline for the same:

Age Limit

The startup should not exceed ten years from the date of incorporation or registration.

Turnover Limit

The annual turnover of the startup during any of the financial years since incorporation shall not have exceeded ₹100 crore.

Innovation Criteria

The business should contribute to innovation and add value to existing products or services, distinguishing it from other companies in the market.

Scalability Potential

Startups must demonstrate high scalability, with the potential to generate substantial wealth and create employment opportunities.

Sector-Specific Requirements

Any sector or industry based startup can avail this facility under the Startup India Scheme except those who qualify for primary eligibility criteria.
